5.3 nth Term of an AP
Let us consider the situation again, given in Section 5.1 in which Reena applied for a job and got selected. She has been offered the job with a starting monthly salary of
` 8000, with an annual increment of ` 500. What would be her monthly salary for the fifth year?
To answer this, let us first see what her monthly salary for the second year would be.
It would be ` (8000 + 500) = ` 8500. In the same way, we can find the monthly salary for the 3rd, 4th and 5th year by adding ` 500 to the salary of the previous year.
So, the salary for the 3rd year = ` (8500 + 500)
= ` (8000 + 500 + 500)
= ` (8000 + 2 × 500)
= ` [8000 + (3 – 1) × 500] (for the 3rd year)
= ` 9000
Salary for the 4th year = ` (9000 + 500)
= ` (8000 + 500 + 500 + 500)
= ` (8000 + 3 × 500)
= ` [8000 + (4 – 1) × 500] (for the 4th year)

Salary for the 5th year = ` (9500 + 500)

= ` (8000 + 4 × 500)
= ` [8000 + (5 – 1) × 500] (for the 5th year)
= ` 10000
Observe that we are getting a list of numbers
8000, 8500, 9000, 9500, 10000, . . .

Let a1 , a2 , a3 , . . . be an AP whose first term a1 is a and the common difference is d.
the second term a2 = a + d = a + (2 – 1) d the third term a3 = a2 + d = (a + d) + d = a + 2d = a + (3 – 1) d the fourth term a4 = a3 + d = (a + 2d) + d = a + 3d = a + (4 – 1) d
