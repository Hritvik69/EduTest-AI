Theorem 1 The number of permutations of n different objects taken r at a time, where 0 < r ≤ n and the objects do not repeat is n ( n – 1) ( n – 2). . .( n – r + 1), which is denoted by nPr.
Proof There will be as many permutations as there are ways of filling in r vacant places . . . by
← r vacant places → the n objects. The first place can be filled in n ways; following which, the second place can be filled in (n – 1) ways, following which the third place can be filled in (n – 2)
ways,..., the rth place can be filled in (n – (r – 1)) ways. Therefore, the number of ways of filling in r vacant places in succession is n(n – 1) (n – 2) . . . (n – (r – 1)) or n ( n – 1) (n – 2) ... (n – r + 1)
