3. MEDIAN
Median is that positional value of the variable which divides the distribution into two equal parts, one part comprises all values greater than or equal to the median value and the other comprises all values less than or equal to it. The Median is the “middle” element when the data set is arranged in order of the magnitude.
Since the median is determined by the position of different values, it remains unaffected if, say, the size of the largest value increases.
Computation of median
The median can be easily computed by sorting the data from smallest to largest and finding out the middle value.

The “middle score” is 6, so the median is 6. Half of the scores are larger than 6 and half of the scores are smaller.
